What is a QR Code?

A QR Code (Quick Response Code) is a two-dimensional barcode that stores information in a pattern of black and white squares. Unlike traditional barcodes that can only hold a limited amount of data, QR codes can store much more information, including website URLs, text, phone numbers, email addresses, payment details, social media profiles, and Wi-Fi credentials.

QR codes were developed in 1994 by Denso Wave, a Japanese company, to improve data tracking in manufacturing. Today, they are widely used across industries such as retail, healthcare, education, logistics, marketing, transportation, and digital payments.

One of the main reasons for the popularity of QR codes is their ease of use. Users simply scan the code using a smartphone camera, and the encoded information becomes instantly available.

How Does a QR Code Work?

A QR code works by converting information into a machine-readable pattern. When a QR code is generated, the data is encoded into a grid of black and white modules. These modules represent binary information that can be interpreted by scanning software.

The process typically works as follows:

  1. A QR code generator converts information into a QR code image.
  2. The QR code is displayed digitally or printed on a physical surface.
  3. A user scans the QR code using a smartphone camera or QR scanner app.
  4. The scanner reads the encoded pattern and decodes the stored information.
  5. The device performs the intended action, such as opening a website, displaying text, connecting to Wi-Fi, or processing a payment.

Modern smartphones include built-in QR scanning functionality, making QR codes more accessible than ever before.
